Conclusions

Analyzing the factors that influence student drop-out and success rates is essential for implementing preventive measures and ensuring every student's success. Our analysis examines the relationship between academic, demographic, and socioeconomic factors and student success rates. Academic performance plays a key role, with better evaluations of curricular programs linked to lower dropout rates. This shows the importance of regularly improving educational programs and reviewing students' individual academic performance to help students stay on track. Demographic factors like gender and marital status also impact dropout rates. Data suggests that certain gender groups face higher dropout rates and marital status can also affect academic performance and dropout decisions, which highlights the need for targeted support to address the challenges these groups may experience. Socioeconomic conditions, such unemployment rates and economic stability, are also closely tied to student outcomes. Students from areas with higher unemployment or lower GDP tend to have higher dropout rates. These findings point to the need for schools to implement intiatives that support students academically, accomodate the challenges different groups face, and address external factors like economic stability to help students succeed.
